The Company

Who We Are

A small company with a large appetite for story. Here are the people who make the work.

Holly Hipwell

Founder

Creative Director

Before co-founding Boudicca Productions, Holly spent her time managing complex logistics and bringing big ideas to life, tackling everything from coordinating global literary campaigns like Miu Miu Summer Reads to building communities in the literature and theatre worlds.

While a childhood spent spellbound by West End shows and films based on real people and society sparked her love for theatre, Holly realized she preferred the puzzle behind the scenes. She thrives on a logistical challenge, which makes her the perfect fit to head up Boudicca’s producing and marketing. For Holly, there is nothing better than taking a messy, ambitious idea and figuring out how all the moving parts fit together to build the bigger picture.

Beyond her laptop, Holly enjoys debating other dog parents as to why Luna is far superior, hunting for the next fantasy novel with a steamy cup of coffee and sweet talking Claude (the AI) into doing her bidding.

Eleanor Wilson

Founder

Creative Director

Before co-founding Boudicca Productions, Eleanor worked across the theatre industry in an array of roles. After graduating from the Guildford School of Acting with a BA in Theatre Production, specialising in Stage Management, she went on to work as an assistant at several leading talent agencies, which she still does and thoroughly enjoys.

She credits her background in stage management with making her an expert wrangler of information, actors and creatives alike, while also fostering an addiction to beautifully organised spreadsheets and even prettier stationery.

Outside of the theatre and office, Eleanor can usually be found blasting her questionable taste in music, rummaging through charity bookshops, or drinking Diet Coke as though it's a life-saving elixir.

Annabel Caldwell

Founder

Creative Associate

Before co-founding Boudicca Productions, Annabel graduated with a BA in Theology and Performing Arts in 2024, gaining experience across acting, directing, writing and production. She later completed the Mercury Theatre's Directors' Course, further refining her directing practice and creative leadership.

Since graduating, Annabel has worked across a range of professional and amateur productions as an assistant director, stage manager, performer and co-director. She is passionate about championing new writing, reimagining classic texts and creating rehearsal rooms where creativity and collaboration can flourish.

Outside the rehearsal room, Annabel can usually be found serving pints at work, adding an unruly number of books to her ever-growing "to be read" pile, or convincing herself that buying just one more Sanrio blind box is a perfectly sensible investment.
